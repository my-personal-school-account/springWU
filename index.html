I have a weak spot for first principles thinking — not because I think it always gets you to the right answer, but because it forces you to be honest about what you actually know versus what you're assuming. A lot of disagreements, when you trace them back far enough, turn out to be disagreements about assumptions rather than conclusions. That's useful to know. I'm also interested in the meta-question of how people and organisations get better at thinking and deciding over time. The answer seems to be less about intelligence and more about having good feedback loops, being willing to revisit decisions, and creating conditions where people can say what they actually think. None of that is complicated in principle. In practice it's surprisingly hard. I try not to have too many fixed opinions about things I haven't spent real time with. The internet rewards confident takes, and I understand the incentive, but I think it's done a lot of damage to the quality of public thinking. Epistemic humility isn't a very exciting trait to broadcast, but I think it's underrated.
